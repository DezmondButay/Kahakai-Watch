Researching Hawaii Coastal Erosion Monitoring With Cyber Infrastructure

Dezmond Butay
STEM Capstone Program — Waipahu High School
november 09, 2025

Abstract

This proposal focuses on studying coastal erosion in Hawai‘i and exploring how cyber infrastructure and digital tools can help monitor and predict shoreline changes...

Introduction

Hawai‘i’s coastlines are under threat from rising sea levels, strong wave activity, and storms...

Background and significance

Coastal erosion occurs when natural forces like waves, tides, and currents remove sand and soil from the shore...

Problem Statement

Currently, Hawai‘i does not have a fully integrated system that tracks coastal erosion over time...

Hypothesis and Goals

Hypothesis: If cyber infrastructure is used to collect, organize, and visualize shoreline data, then it can help researchers and planners better predict erosion patterns and support coastal protection efforts.

  1. Collect and organize data from publicly available shoreline and sea level rise resources.
  2. Create a digital framework to visualize erosion patterns over time.
  3. Identify high-risk areas and explore ways cyber infrastructure can improve planning for communities and natural habitats.
  4. Provide a user-friendly system accessible by schools, local agencies, and the public.

Aims & Potential Pitfalls

Aim 1: Data Collection and Baseline Creation
Collect shoreline, ocean conditions, and environmental data to create a baseline for erosion. potential pitfalls: cloud cover, temporal gaps; alternatives: drone imagery and interpolation.

Aim 2: Visualization and Cause Identification
Use interactive maps and time-lapse visualizations; pitfalls: misaligned coordinates; alternatives: careful georeferencing & machine learning to identify patterns.

Aim 3: Future Erosion Prediction
Build predictive models and scenario analysis; pitfalls: uncertainty in climate projections; alternatives: scenario based modelling and uncertainty quantification.
