Researching Hawaii Coastal Erosion Monitoring With Cyber Infrastructure

Dezmond Butay
STEM Capstone Program — Waipahu High School
November 09, 2025

Abstract

This proposal focuses on studying coastal erosion in Hawai‘i and exploring how cyber infrastructure and digital tools can help monitor and predict shoreline changes.

Introduction

Hawai‘i’s coastlines are under threat from rising sea levels, strong wave activity, and storms.

Background and Significance

Coastal erosion occurs when natural forces like waves, tides, and currents remove sand and soil from the shore.

Problem Statement

Hawai‘i does not yet have a fully integrated system that tracks coastal erosion consistently over time.

Hypothesis and Goals

Hypothesis: Using cyber infrastructure to collect and visualize shoreline data will improve erosion prediction and coastal planning.

  1. Collect shoreline and sea level rise data.
  2. Visualize erosion patterns over time.
  3. Identify high-risk coastal areas.
  4. Provide public access to erosion data.
