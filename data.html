Kahakai Watch

Monitoring Shoreline Change Across Hawaiʻi

Project Overview

Kahakai Watch analyzes shoreline change across Hawaiʻi using historical coastal datasets and aerial imagery. The project measures long-term shoreline movement to identify beaches experiencing erosion and areas where shorelines remain stable.

Data Preparation

Historical shoreline positions were compiled from aerial imagery and coastal surveys spanning several decades. Each dataset includes estimated positional uncertainty values which help ensure accurate shoreline comparisons between different years.

Methodology

Shoreline change rates were calculated using transects placed perpendicular to the coastline. These transects measure the distance between shoreline positions across time to estimate long-term shoreline movement in feet per year.

Analysis

Data visualization tools such as bar charts and maps were used to compare shoreline change rates between Hawaiian Islands. These visualizations help identify regional erosion patterns and highlight locations experiencing rapid shoreline retreat.

Key Findings

Beaches on Oʻahu show the highest erosion rates, with several locations experiencing shoreline retreat of over three feet per year. Maui and Kauaʻi generally show moderate erosion, while some beaches remain relatively stable due to sediment movement and coastal conditions.

Conclusion

The results demonstrate that shoreline change varies widely across Hawaiʻi. By combining historical shoreline data with modern visualization tools, Kahakai Watch provides accessible information that can support coastal research, education, and long-term shoreline management.
