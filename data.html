Tracking Hawai‘i’s Shoreline Change & Coastal Erosion

Explore historical shoreline data, analyze erosion patterns, and visualize long-term coastal change across Hawai‘i’s islands using transects and shoreline measurements.

Project Overview

Kahakai Watch analyzes shoreline change across Hawai‘i using historical coastal datasets and aerial imagery.

Data Preparation

Shoreline positions compiled from aerial imagery and coastal surveys over decades, including uncertainty estimates.

Methodology

Transects perpendicular to the coastline measure distance between shoreline positions to calculate erosion rates.

Analysis

Charts and maps help identify regional erosion patterns and highlight high-risk areas.

Key Findings

Oʻahu shows the fastest erosion; Maui and Kauaʻi moderate, while some areas remain stable due to sediment dynamics.

Conclusion

Combining datasets and visualization tools supports research, education, and long-term shoreline management.
